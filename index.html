How I Build

Three pillars

Most engineers focus on whether something works. I care about three equally important experiences:

◎

User Experience

Can someone accomplish their goal without confusion, frustration, or needing to read documentation?

⟨/⟩

Developer Experience

Can future developers understand, maintain, and extend the system without accumulating unnecessary technical debt?

⚙

Operational Experience

Can the product be managed and maintained without creating an entire job dedicated to keeping it alive?

The best products don't optimize for only one audience. They make life easier for everyone involved.

Things I Believe

Principles that guide every decision

Software should feel obvious before it feels powerful.

Documentation is important, but great products should not require it.

Complexity is often accidental, not necessary.

The customer journey matters as much as the code.

The developer experience matters as much as the user experience.

Requirements are the starting point, not the finish line.

Done is better than perfect, but delightful is better than merely done.

Every product should make someone's day a little easier.

Philosophy

My differentiator isn't that I can build software. It's that I can identify friction across an entire system — customers, developers, operators, businesses — and design solutions that make life easier for everyone involved.

Requirements are important, but they're only the starting point. Anyone can implement a checklist. The real work begins after:

  • What feels confusing?
  • What can be automated?
  • What decisions can be made on behalf of the user?
  • What future maintenance burden are we creating?
  • What unnecessary complexity are we asking people to carry?

I believe great products come from personally walking through every step of the experience and relentlessly removing friction. The technology is rarely the point. The people are.

Family Connection

Bivvi

A shared reading video call app for grandparents and grandchildren who live far apart.

For years I struggled with a simple problem: how do I help my parents build a meaningful relationship with my children when they don't live nearby? Traditional video calls felt awkward and unstructured. Storytime already existed as a meaningful family ritual, so instead of adapting storytime to video calls, I adapted video calls to storytime.

Bivvi helps distant family members connect through shared experience — because the core message isn't "shared reading." It's helping grandparents and grandchildren stay connected through moments that matter.

Sales Enablement

Summa

AI-assisted call follow-up for sales teams: summaries, action items, CRM notes, and clean emails from real conversations.

A close friend worked at a demanding startup and spent enormous amounts of time managing follow-ups, timelines, customer information, CRM updates, and admin work after meetings. I wanted to reduce that burden so he could spend less time documenting work and more time being present with his wife and daughter.

The core message isn't about AI. It's about giving people more time for the things that matter. The AI is simply the mechanism.

Radio Programming

TxR Studio

A modern, web-first radio programming tool that turns telecom configuration into plain-language guided setup.

CHIRP is an excellent tool, but many radio users aren't experts. There are setup decisions that can reasonably be automated, concepts that can be explained more clearly, and workflows that can be simplified.

TxR Studio transforms radio programming from a technical exercise into a guided experience — helping people focus on communication instead of configuration.

Faith & Community

Everyday Chaplet

A gentle prayer companion for people exploring prayer who find one of my handmade rosary bracelets and want a simple place to begin.

I wanted prayer to become a more natural part of my day, but there were times when I struggled to find uninterrupted time for an entire rosary. The one-decade chaplet bracelet emerged from a simple realization: prayer doesn't need to happen in a perfectly scheduled block of time.

It can happen whenever an opportunity presents itself. The goal isn't to replace devotion — it's to lower the barrier to participation.

Industry Podcast

The GRC Podcast

Practical conversations with security champions about governance, risk, and compliance — how it actually gets done, not just how it looks on paper.

The security industry spends a tremendous amount of time discussing theory, frameworks, and requirements. Those things matter. But eventually security has to function inside real organizations with limited budgets, limited time, limited staffing, and competing priorities.

I created the podcast to challenge the perception that GRC exists to create roadblocks. Security teams should be partners to engineering. The goal isn't to create speed bumps. It's to make secure decisions the easiest decisions.
