How I Think See Examples
Approach

What I believe

01

Complexity should disappear, not get documented

Great products don't explain how hard they are to use. They remove the friction so well that the user never needs to understand what was broken.

02

Requirements are a starting point, not a finish line

People struggle in ways they can't articulate. I start by observing where the real friction lives, then redesign from there — even if it means rethinking the brief entirely.

03

Experience matters on both sides

User experience and developer experience are equally important. Systems should be enjoyable for customers, maintainable for developers, and sustainable for operators.

04

Every project starts with a problem worth solving

I don't build software to see what sticks. Every project begins by identifying a specific point of struggle and designing a better experience around it.

Philosophy

My differentiator isn't that I can build software. It's that I can identify friction throughout the entire customer journey and remove it.

Across every project I work on, the through-line is the same: simplifying complex systems without sacrificing capability. The goal isn't to build features — it's to make the parts that used to be difficult simply not difficult anymore. That's the pattern that repeats across video communication, sales enablement, radio programming, faith-based tools, and everything else.
